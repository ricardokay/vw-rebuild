Album Review

Alvvays Compress an Entire Architecture of Feeling Into Four Perfect Minutes

Review by Marcus Reid  ·  June 10, 2026

Alvvays performing at the Rickshaw Theatre, Vancouver. Photo: Jennifer McInnis

Alvvays

Blue Rev

Polyvinyl  ·  2022  ·  Dream pop

Essential
8.8 / 10

There's a particular kind of efficiency to the best Alvvays songs — the feeling that they've arrived from somewhere else fully formed, that no revision was needed, that Molly Rankin just opened a door and let them in. Blue Rev is full of this feeling: twelve tracks that shimmer and careen and ache in equal measure, none of them overstaying their welcome, all of them leaving you wanting another three minutes of exactly that.

Recorded with production from Shawn Everett and John Agnello, the album finds the Toronto band expanding their sonic palette without betraying what makes them essential. The guitars are fuzzier, the synths more saturated, the tempos more varied — but the melodies are still those melodies, each one a small miracle of compression, the kind that lodges immediately and refuses to leave for days.

"Pharmacist" opens the record at a sprint, Rankin's voice cutting through a wall of distortion to deliver what might be the year's most indelible opening couplet. From there the album moves with the logic of a great mixtape: confident, slightly restless, always moving toward something.
