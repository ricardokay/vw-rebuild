A La Music

The Commodore Ballroom at 95: How Vancouver's Greatest Venue Became the Heartbeat of the City's Cultural Soul

By Sarah Chen June 13, 2026
[ featured image ]

There is a particular kind of magic that only old rooms possess — the accumulated resonance of ten thousand nights, the invisible weight of every band that ever tuned up on the low stage and looked out at the sprung dance floor below. The Commodore Ballroom on Granville Street has this quality in abundance, and at 95 years old it remains the most important room in Vancouver music history.

It has outlasted empires of taste. The Commodore survived big band, folk revival, punk, new wave, grunge, and the entire streaming era without once losing the quality that every serious musician says they feel the moment they walk onstage: the sense that the room itself is listening.

Album Review
Alvvays

Blue Rev

9.2/10

On their third album, Alvvays compress an entire architecture of feeling into four minutes at a time — jangling guitar runs that cascade like rain on a skylight, Molly Rankin's voice suspended somewhere between heartbreak and irony, the whole thing mixed loud enough to fill your chest but detailed enough to reward headphones at midnight.
