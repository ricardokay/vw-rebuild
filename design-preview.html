Alvvays Compress an Entire Architecture of Feeling Into Four Perfect Minutes

Review by Marcus Reid · June 10, 2026
Alvvays
Blue Rev
Essential
8.8 / 10

On their third album, Alvvays compress an entire architecture of feeling into four minutes at a time — jangling guitar runs that cascade like rain on a skylight, Molly Rankin's voice suspended somewhere between heartbreak and irony, the whole thing mixed loud enough to fill your chest but detailed enough to reward headphones at midnight.

Blue Rev is the rare album that sounds effortless because every effort has been hidden. The hooks land exactly when you stop expecting them. The production choices — the reverb dialled to the edge of wash without losing definition, the drums compressed into a tight, satisfying thump — are invisible in exactly the way great production should be.
